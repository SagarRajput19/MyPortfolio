Fraud Awareness

Learn to recognize and protect yourself from financial scams

Phishing Scams

Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by pretending to be a trustworthy entity in electronic communication.

How to protect yourself: Never click on links in unsolicited emails, always verify the sender's address, and never provide personal information via email.

Ponzi Schemes

Fraudulent investment operations where returns are paid to earlier investors using the capital of newer investors.

Red flags: Promises of high returns with little risk, overly consistent returns, unlicensed sellers, secretive strategies, and difficulty receiving payments.

Identity Theft

When someone steals your personal information to commit fraud, such as opening accounts or making purchases in your name.

Prevention: Shred documents with personal information, use strong passwords, monitor your credit reports, and be cautious with sharing personal data online.
