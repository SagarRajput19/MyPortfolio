Budgeting Basics

Budgeting is the foundation of personal finance. It's about understanding how much money you have coming in and where it's going out.

The 50/30/20 Rule

A simple budgeting method where you allocate:

  • 50% of income to needs (rent, food, utilities)
  • 30% to wants (entertainment, dining out)
  • 20% to savings and debt repayment

Budgeting Tools

Consider using budgeting apps or simple spreadsheets to track your expenses. The key is consistency.

Saving Strategies

Building savings is crucial for financial security and achieving your life goals.

Pay Yourself First

Automatically transfer a portion of every paycheck to savings before you spend on anything else.

Emergency Fund

Aim to save 3-6 months' worth of living expenses for unexpected situations like job loss or medical emergencies.

Fraud Awareness

Recognize and protect yourself from common financial scams

Phishing Scams

Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by pretending to be a trustworthy entity.

Ponzi Schemes

Fraudulent investment operations paying returns to earlier investors with new investors' money.

Identity Theft

Stealing personal information to commit fraud or theft in your name.

Credit Card Fraud

Unauthorized use of credit card information to make purchases or withdraw funds.

Phishing Scams

Phishing is when scammers try to trick you into giving out personal information such as your bank account numbers, passwords, and credit card numbers.

How to Recognize Phishing

  • Urgent or threatening language in emails/messages
  • Requests for personal information
  • Links to suspicious websites
  • Spelling and grammar mistakes

Protection Tips

  • Never click links in suspicious emails
  • Always verify the sender's email address
  • Use two-factor authentication
  • Keep your software updated
